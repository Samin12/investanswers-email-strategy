## SpaceX IPO (SPCX) — Key Details
- **IPO price:** Fixed at **$135/share** (no range — unusual; signaled deal was pre-covered). Priced June 11.
- **Listing:** **Friday, June 12, 2026** on Nasdaq, ticker **SPCX**.
- **Size:** 555.6M Class A shares = **$75B raise** — the biggest IPO in history, >3x Alibaba's record.
- **Valuation:** **$1.77 trillion** — would rank ~7th largest US company, above Tesla (~$1.6T).
- **Demand:** Reported **>$250B in orders, ~3.5–4x oversubscribed**.
- **Control:** Musk retains >82% voting control post-offering.
- **Day-1 expectations:** Significant first-day pop and volatility expected (commentators cite Figma's +250% day-1 as the hot-IPO template), but analysts widely warn against buying the open — historically >90% of IPOs later trade below their first-day low, and hot IPOs tend to underperform the S&P long-run. Cramer (Jun 11) said this IPO "will determine market action for the next month."

## Macro Summary

**1. Inflation is hot — May CPI 4.2% (released June 10).** Headline CPI +0.5% m/m, **+4.2% y/y — highest since April 2023**, up from 2.4% in January. Energy drove >60% of the monthly increase: energy +3.9% m/m / +23.5% y/y, gasoline **+40.5% y/y**. Core CPI was tamer: +0.2% m/m, 2.9% y/y. The spike is overwhelmingly an oil shock from the Iran war and Strait of Hormuz closure.

**2. Fed: holds at 3.50%–3.75%, but markets price HIKES later in 2026.** April FOMC held; minutes showed a majority of officials see "higher rates might become necessary." June 16-17 meeting (under new Chair **Kevin Warsh**) is priced ~98% no-change, but prediction markets price the first **hike** at the October meeting (32%) or September (26%); one tally put cumulative 2026 hike odds at ~63% after the strong May jobs report + 4.2% CPI. Rate-cut bets for 2026 are essentially dead.

**3. The early-June Nasdaq selloff — confirmed.** Friday **June 5**: Nasdaq **-4.18%** (worst day since April 2025), Nasdaq-100 -4.77%, S&P 500 -2.64% to 7,383.74, Dow -695 pts — triggered by a much stronger-than-expected May jobs report (spiking yields/hike odds), Broadcom's failure to raise AI guidance, a skeptical AI research report, and a Meta secondary offering; ~$1T wiped from semis. A second leg down hit **June 9-10** after Trump teased new Iran strikes; Nasdaq bottomed June 10 at 25,169 (-7.1% from June 2 record of 27,094).

**4. June 11 rebound.** Trump said he canceled planned strikes on Iran and a deal was close: Dow +900 pts (+1.86% to 50,849), S&P +1.75%, Nasdaq +2.54%. WTI fell back below $90 (settled $85.84), 10Y yield dropped to 4.46%. Micron +12%, Intel +10% (upgrade), semis led. Oracle fell despite an earnings beat on guidance/spending concerns.

**5. Iran-Israel: the dominant macro variable.** Months of conflict have left the **Strait of Hormuz (~20% of global oil) at a near-standstill** — the IEA calls it "the largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market." A halt to hostilities was called ~June 8-9 but both sides left the door open to resumption; Trump teased then canceled new strikes June 9-11. Kalshi traders put 66% odds Hormuz traffic doesn't normalize until January 2027. Oil headline risk remains the swing factor for CPI, the Fed, and equities into the June 16-17 FOMC and the June 12 SpaceX debut.
