 Nic Carter was a prominent Bitcoin advocate, GP at Castle Island Ventures, and co-founder of Coin Metrics. He's long been a Bitcoin maximalist voice — bullish on BTC, often critical of altcoins (including Solana

 Since late 2025, he's gone extremely hard on quantum computing as the biggest long-term risk to Bitcoin (and ECC-based chains generally). He wrote a multi-part Substack series ("Bitcoin and the Quantum Problem"), did podcasts (e.g., Bankless: "Bitcoin Has 3 Years to Survive the Quantum Threat"), and repeatedly argued elliptic curve cryptography (ECDSA/Schnorr on secp256k1, used by Bitcoin) is vulnerable to Shor's algorithm on a sufficiently powerful quantum computer. 

 But the real story is Project Eleven - HIS INVESTMENT Castle Island Ventures (Nic's firm) led a $20M Series A into Project Eleven (P11) in January 2026 at a $120M valuation. Nic has been very public about it and is closely involved (board/advisory level per disclosures). Project Eleven is explicitly a post-quantum cryptography infrastructure company for digital assets - aka the more Quantum FUD he spreads the better his bags do. 

 So yes — Nic isn't just opining. His firm has a direct financial stake in the quantum-risk narrative gaining traction across crypto. Heightened fear/urgency helps his bags. 

 The Solana/Anza situation nic_carter never spoke with anyone at Anza. Someone with actual engineering skin in the game is calling out Nic's claims as uninformed. it's refreshing to see technical pushback.
