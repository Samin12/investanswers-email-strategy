 3 parts to this story and none of it is good news

 1. JObs The U.S. added 172,000 jobs in May — roughly double economist expectations of ~80k–85k. Unemployment held steady at 4.3%, and prior months were revised higher.

 This is a classic " good news is bad news " scenario for stocks:

 A strong labor market reduces the odds of Fed rate cuts.

 Investors are now pricing in higher-for-longer interest rates .

 Treasury yields jumped sharply (10-year yield moved above 4.5%).

 Higher yields hurt stock valuations, especially for high-growth, high-multiple tech stocks.
