 The US has higher inequality. The rich (especially the top 1%) capture a larger share of total income and wealth. The gap between the top and bottom is wider.

 Europe redistributes more through higher taxes and stronger social programs. This makes society more equal — the rich don't get quite as rich, and the bottom gets a bigger safety net.

 The chart shows income adjusted by territory: 

 Typical American (ignoring the richest 1%): $72,000 per year

 Typical European: $51,000 per year

 Poorest 20% of Americans: $42,000

 Poorest 20% of Europeans: $32,000

 Standout for me is how close China is ...20 years ago it was a very different story.
