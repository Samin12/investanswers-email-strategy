 Starbucks spends $400 million per year on software and is now building custom in-house AI tools to replace specific Microsoft (inventory tracking) and IBM (maintenance) systems. Some replacements could launch by 2027 as part of broader cost-cutting. The news triggered drops in IBM (3%) and Salesforce (~4%) shares.

 Is this SaaSpocalypse 2.0?

 Partially yes

 AI has materially shifted the build vs. buy economics:

 AI-assisted development (coding agents, better tooling, faster iteration) makes custom systems cheaper and faster to build and maintain than before.

 For a company the size of Starbucks, replacing even a couple of high-cost, imperfect enterprise tools with tailored in-house versions can deliver real savings and better fit.

 This creates credible pressure on legacy or point-solution vendors whose products are clunky or overpriced at enterprise scale.

 Starbucks is showing you what other companies will be doing over the next several years.

 Your job is to position yourself to facilitate that process for as many companies as you can.
