 Jacob shows us how to snipe and avoid downdrafts: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2y0_vumLijw 

 1. Find Good Spots to Buy - aka Zoom out - Start with the Daily Chart Look at the daily chart for key support levels, like the 50-day or 200-day moving average.

 Only focus on stocks you already like that are dropping hard into those levels.

 The bigger the sell-off into support, the better your odds of catching a bounce.

 2. Time Your Entry on the 2-Minute Chart [for the record I (this is IA james) used to use the 2 min chart on Crypto and the 3 min chart on Equities) Switch to the 2-minute chart once price reaches your support zone.

 Wait for a rare all-orange day (heavy selling all day).

 Buy when the color flips from orange to blue. This signals the selling pressure is easing.

 You can buy on the first flip and add more if it holds (great for stacking shares).

 3. Protect Yourself and Skip Bad Trades If the color flips back to orange, exit fast — this acts as your stop. I REPEAT EXIT FAST 

 Ignore signals that flip back and forth with no real upward move.

 Only trust the flip after a big sell-off into a clear support level.
