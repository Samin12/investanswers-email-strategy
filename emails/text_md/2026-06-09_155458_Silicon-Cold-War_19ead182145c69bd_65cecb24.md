 The US and China are in a deepening fight over advanced computer chips and AI technology. Taiwan is considering tougher rules to stop advanced AI chips from being sold or smuggled into China. This would align Taiwan more closely with US policy.

 China is pushing back hard. They’re reportedly planning to spend around $295 billion building data centers across the country and want 80% of the hardware to come from Chinese companies (like Huawei). The clear goal is to reduce reliance on American chips and eventually push companies like NVDA and AMD out of the Chinese market.

 At the same time, the Pentagon added Alibaba (BABA), Baidu (BIDU), and Chinese EV maker BYD (BYDDY) to its official list of Chinese military-linked companies. Being officially labeled as Chinese military companies by the Pentagon can trigger selling by US investors and funds. It also raises the risk of more restrictions or delisting concerns down the road. Alibaba’s plan to sue may limit some damage, but the news is still seen as bearish.

 Note I have a position in BABA but wil monitor carefully.
