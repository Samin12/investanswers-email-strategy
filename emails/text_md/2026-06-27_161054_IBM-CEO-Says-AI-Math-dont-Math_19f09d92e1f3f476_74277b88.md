 TLDR IBM CEO Arvind Krishna: Big AI Models Will Become Cheap Commodities, Massive Spending on Data Centers Won't Pay Off, and Quantum Computing Is the Next Bet 

 Details IBM is already seeing strong results from using AI. They expect to save more than $5 billion in efficiency gains by year 4 compared to their 2022 costs. Their developers are now 40% more productive . The return on investment turns from negative to 10x by year two — not year one.

 Krishna believes foundation models (the big base AI models like GPT) will become basic commodities within 1 to 3 years . This is not a distant risk — it’s IBM’s working assumption today. IBM is not trying to build or own its own foundation model. Instead, they’re betting on the layer that sits on top and connects/manages these models. WOW

 THE FUN PART: Planned AI data centers will need 125 gigawatts of power. This would require $8–12 trillion in spending. To justify that cost, the AI industry would need an extra $4 trillion in revenue. There is no realistic path to get there. THIS IS EXACTLY WHY I THINK PLAYERS LIKE ORACLE ARE A HOUSE OF CARDS...
