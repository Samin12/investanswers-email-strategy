 After 6 years of applying steady discipline Patreon Member Marcus Aureliuse and others around him just reached financial freedom. No lottery tickets. Just consistent, grown-up rules. Now he’s cruising on a retirement at a super young 40 on a roadtrip to Starbase for a SpaceX visit. Living proof the Rules work. 🚀

 Top 3 Rule takeaways: 1. Slow down and keep learning like your future depends on it Markets scream at you daily. Ignore the noise. Stay insatiably curious. 

 2. Less is more. Play smart, not often Think football, not slot machines. Sometimes offense, sometimes defense — but every move calculated. He made most of his money from just two smart plays (SOL run → TSLA at $150). The IA “3 rocks in your pocket” rule is gold: focus deeply on fewer things. Overtrading is the fastest way to give it back. 

 3. Build your own conviction and stay resilient Never borrow someone else’s belief — know exactly why you own what you own. 

 h.t to Maximus 

 His Note: "Hope it’s ok for me to share this here. @InvestAnswers has been a tremendous help along my investing journey. A few tips I’ve learned over 6 years that has allowed me and a few others to become financially free. Sorry if it’s long. 

 Slow down. Sometimes it feels like a race but markets are noisy. Daily content and opinions can be overbearing at times!

 Learn learn learn. Being insatiably curious is paramount to success. You have to level up with your wealth, otherwise you’ll end up losing it all. There’s a ton of books out there on Bitcoin. Read. Sign up for paid substacks. Education isn’t an expense, it’s an investment.

 Markets can be like American football. Sometimes you play offense, sometimes defense, sometimes you throw a Hail Mary or kick a 3 pointer. Though every decision should be calculated. Not Hail Marys every trade or perpetually play defense.

 Resilience. Mistakes happen. Looking past mistakes and moving on is key. Don’t give up no matter what. Brighter days will come.

 Expect the unexpected. For me, my big win came from Oct 2023 SOL run. Then rotating that into TSLA at $150. I’ve overtraded in the past, but the majority of my success came from two trades. Two!

 Live a simple life. Learn to be happy with less. There’s peace in that. Sometimes, more stuff = more headaches.

 Plan plan plan. Can’t express how important this is. Plan for what-ifs so when they happen, you know exactly what to do. Spreadsheets, apps, models. Just make sure you aren’t overanalyzing (analysis paralysis). It’s a real thing.

 Never lean on someone for conviction. Leaning on someone else’s conviction is doomed to fail. You have to know what you hold. When the bull begins to buck, conviction is your saddle.

 Less is more. I love James analogy of 3 rocks in your pocket. Each rock is a trade. This makes you think more. Develop patience. Not easy.

 Be nice. We are all human and make mistakes. The journey is just as fun as the destination."
