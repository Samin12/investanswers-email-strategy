 Rem cancer impacts 40% of us. 

 This is one of the first concrete examples of moving from early detection to true primary prevention for a major cancer, by identifying at-risk people years ahead and targeting the underlying inflammatory mechanism (especially linked to air pollution). Topol frames it as a potential template for other cancers. 

 A validated 14-protein blood test can predict lung cancer risk more than 5 years before diagnosis Researchers used high-throughput proteomics + machine learning on nearly 3,000 proteins in over 48,000 UK Biobank participants and identified a 14-protein signature that predicts lung cancer ~5.6 years in advance. It works in both smokers and never-smokers, outperforms existing risk models, and was validated across 8 independent cohorts (including never-smokers in Taiwan). Importantly, these proteins come from stressed healthy bystander cells , not the tumor itself.
