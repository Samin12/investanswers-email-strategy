 OK it was a pretty crappy call but it contained lots of very positive nuggets. 

 Profits from selling cars stayed about the same, even though more buyers are choosing the Full Self-Driving (FSD) software upgrade. 

 Energy Storage Profits were lower than normal because of some temporary issues. They should settle back around a healthy 25% going forward.

 Robotaxi (Self-Driving Taxi) Progress — some truly impressive updates:

 Tesla has already completed more than 380,000 miles of unsupervised Robotaxi driving (no human safety driver in the car) with zero notable incidents. This is one of the strongest real-world safety proofs Tesla has ever shared for fully driverless operation.

 Testing miles are growing fast — by double-digit percentages every week. To support this pace, Tesla expects to have roughly 5,000 cars in the Robotaxi test fleet by the end of the year.

 The current test fleet (using both the new dedicated Cybercab and regular Model Y vehicles) is already running early versions of the next big software update: FSD Version 15. They're actively improving it with several planned tracks.

 Tesla has no plans to partner with Uber or Lyft. They will run their own independent Robotaxi network. 

 FSD is already helping sell more new Teslas in the U.S. and other places where it’s approved for use.

 Upgrades for Older Cars are still planned if they had FSD. 

 Tesla Semi Truck

 Work on making the Semi self-driving (FSD for trucks) is expected to start toward the end of 2026 or early 2027. They’re being careful not to rush it or distract from perfecting safety on passenger cars and the Cybercab first.

 Production of the Semi trucks themselves is still ramping up slowly.

 Optimus Humanoid Robot

 Production of the Optimus robot will also ramp up very slowly at first. We know but the new line is impressive. 

 Optimus is expected to be special: it should be the only robot that can truly “see” and understand the world around it (using advanced vision) and perform complex tasks the way a human would.

 Major New Factory: Terafab

 Tesla will soon make a big announcement about Terafab (or share more details on its location/plans). This is a massive new project. It will combine chip design, manufacturing, memory, and packaging under one roof — critical for powering self-driving tech, Optimus, and AI training. 

 Solar and Energy Storage

 Tesla’s solar panel manufacturing factory is coming online sooner than previously expected. It will mainly supply panels for Tesla’s own large solar projects and “Tesla farms.”

 Memory and Micron

 Supply Chain — Tesla has secured a significant supply of important memory chips from Micron at good prices for the coming years - this is critical for Optimus as it needs 4x the memory of a car. 

 SpaceX Merger

 When asked about a possible merger (likely related to xAI rumors), they dodged a direct answer, but the overall tone felt more positive (“yes”) than negative.
