 A lot of so-called experts out there are claiming a merger between SpaceX and Tesla is imminent. I don’t buy it. In my view, any potential tie-up is still years down the road. However, the common theory is that if they do merge, it will be a "merger of equals"—meaning both companies would combine at matching market caps. WHY? Because Tesla shareholders will reject it unless merger of equals - myself inckuded. 

 If we assume that hypothetical scenario played out today, matching Tesla's valuation to SpaceX's current market cap would put Tesla stock at exactly $753 per share. This is what I refer to as the arbitrage—or "arb"—opportunity, though it's strictly a longer-term play. think late 2027 or 2028. I am happy to be wrong on this timeline.
