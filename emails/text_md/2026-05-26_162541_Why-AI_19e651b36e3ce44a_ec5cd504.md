 In the AI age, computing power is power. Data centers are the new factories that will decide who leads in technology, medicine, defense, robotics, and manufacturing. Countries that can't build them fast will become dependent on those who can.

 China and Russia don't let activists, zoning boards, climate groups, or endless permits slow them down. They just build the power plants and data centers they need. The U.S. and other free countries, however, often block their own progress with bureaucracy and anti-growth rules.

 The real danger isn't that governments will use AI to spy on people. The bigger danger is that we stop building AI infrastructure altogether, handing dominance to authoritarian regimes that don't care about civil liberties or public debate.

 I wish politicians could understand this very basic arithmetic.
