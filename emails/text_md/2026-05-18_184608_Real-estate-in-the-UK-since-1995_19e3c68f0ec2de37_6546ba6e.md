 All my life real estate was the safe compounder. Well that math just changed. 

 Real estate in the UK since 2007 has been a masterclass in "you went nowhere, but it felt like you did." 

 On paper? "House has gone up £100k+! Property is amazing!"

 In reality (after the inflation thief has done his work)? Real house prices are flat to slightly negative — around -2% according to solid analysis from Savills using Nationwide data.

 How to Optimize the Real Estate Trade If you bought in 1995 and sold in 2007, that was one of the best real estate trades you could have made in modern UK history. Buy for £60K and sell for £180K after 12 years 

 Another example You buy a house for £184,000 in 2007. Today it's "worth" £270,000. But the £184,000 you originally put in now has the same buying power as roughly £265k–£270k+ today because of cumulative inflation. The "profit" vanished into thin air (or more accurately, into higher prices for everything else ).
