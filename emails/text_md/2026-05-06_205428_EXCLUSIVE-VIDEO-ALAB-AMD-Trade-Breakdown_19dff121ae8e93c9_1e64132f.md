 Rem if I am hedging it maybe a signal for you to trim if you do not play options eg if you are tax free ... sell 10 to 20% and buy back in lower... if you have short term capital gains this may not work. 

 I repeatedly hammers this:

 Daily mean reversion backtest = 100% win rate in his data

 When things are “really really really hot” → statistically the highest probability of pullback or sideways chop

 AMD example: 20% gap up on earnings → classic setup for a gap fill + consolidation (he’s already seen 13-15% pullbacks in similar situations)

 Key technical signals: Mean reversion is the bible. 

 4-hour trend indicator: if it flips orange, trim or hedge.

 10-day moving average: ride the trend while price stays above it; if it breaks after a huge run, the move may be ending.

 LILO levels: the higher the level, the more extended the stock is. ALAB and AMD are both in elevated zones, which makes hedging more attractive.

 Rolling calls: if the stock blows past the strike, buy back the call and sell a higher one, possibly further out in time.
