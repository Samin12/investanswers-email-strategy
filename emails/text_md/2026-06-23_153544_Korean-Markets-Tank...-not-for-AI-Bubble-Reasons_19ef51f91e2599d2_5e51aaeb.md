 South Korea’s ruling party just proposed taxing “unrealized gains” on stocks and real estate. This means investors would pay taxes every year on paper profits — the rise in value of assets they still own — even if they haven’t sold anything.

 This idea sparked a sharp selloff in the Korean stock market today, with some already calling it “Black Tuesday.”

 The Netherlands tried almost the exact same policy in February 2026. It passed a 36% tax on unrealized gains for stocks, bonds, and crypto. The backlash was instant (over 61,000 signatures on a petition and strong criticism from business leaders like Shopify’s CEO), and the government quickly scrapped the unrealized gains part 

 These wealth taxes are also controversial and have led some wealthy people to move countries in the past.
