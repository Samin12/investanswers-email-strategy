 AI just took a sledgehammer to the old consulting pyramid. If you are in this realm time to start your own shop! 

 For decades the big firms — McKinsey, BCG, Bain, Big Four — ran a simple model: a few expensive partners at the top, leveraged by armies of juniors doing the research, modeling, and slide work. You pay $5M for a slidedeck created by a 23 year old. That’s how they scaled and charged millions.

 The numbers are shifting fast. PwC just cut 5,600 jobs — their first headcount cut since 2010 — and scrapped a plan to add 100,000 people this year. Revenue growth slowed while they’re still figuring out the new world.

 Meanwhile, small AI-native boutiques are eating their lunch. Some are growing 2-3x faster than the giants. We’re seeing 3-to-10 person teams win Fortune 500 work directly from McKinsey. One example: a tiny crew processed 10 years of client data in 48 hours and delivered the strategy for ~$200k instead of the usual $4M+ six-week project.

 Why the small guy has an oppty AI collapses the junior layer. What used to take months of analysts now happens in hours.

 Speed + lower cost = better ROI for clients who are done overpaying for leverage they no longer need.

 The small firms are laser-focused, and built around AI from day one instead of bolting it on later.

 David vs Golaith The Davids don’t need 50 juniors. The Goliaths still do.
