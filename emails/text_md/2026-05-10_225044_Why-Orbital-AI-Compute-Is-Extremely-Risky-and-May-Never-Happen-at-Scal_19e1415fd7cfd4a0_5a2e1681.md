 A lot of folks think SpaceXAI is the better play than tesla. But SpaceX is risky. 

 SpaceX (and Musk personally) has promoted orbital data centers as a “no-brainer” for cheap, abundant solar-powered AI compute—potentially the lowest-cost location within 2–3 years. 

 However, check this from the S1:

 In early 2026, SpaceX filed with the FCC for up to 1 million satellites dedicated to an “Orbital Data Center” system. Yet the company’s confidential S-1 pre-IPO filing (reviewed by Reuters in April 2026) contains unusually blunt risk disclosures:

 “Our initiatives to develop orbital AI compute and in-orbit, lunar, and interplanetary industrialization are in early stages, involve significant technical complexity and unproven technologies, and may not achieve commercial viability .” Any future orbital infrastructure will operate “in the harsh and unpredictable environment of space, exposing [it] to a wide and unique range of space-related risks that could cause [it] to malfunction or fail.”

 So there is a lot of risk and what I said back in Jan SpaceX and Space Based Data Centers may not really be viable until 2028-2030. The rest is Starlink and Speculation.
