 Hormuz threatening to reopen, Oil Tanking fell from 103 to 88 - as Tankers could flow again. 

 But why does this impact the bond market? 

 Simple

 A credible deal that reopens Hormuz = massive increase in global oil supply (Iran ~3–4M bpd potential).

 Lower oil prices → lower inflation expectations → lower Treasury yields (higher bond prices).

 So when the 10Y keeps threatening to break 4.40% (a level that often acts as a cap in this cycle), a “deal leak” conveniently appears and gives the market an excuse to sell the yield back down.
