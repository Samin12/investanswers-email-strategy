 a quick story of being early + conviction + patience beats almost everything else in investing. The later you arrive, the smaller your slice of the pie becomes 

 The SpaceX Wealth Story SpaceX is worth roughly $1.75 trillion privately. Because Elon Musk gave real stock to the people actually building the rockets, thousands of employees have become millionaires — and hundreds are worth $100 million or more and a few billionaires.

 I used AI to put it into a chart - the earlier you joined in the timeline the more money you made. 

 A small group of very early people (co-founders and top execs who joined 2002–2008) are now billionaires or half-billionaires.

 Roughly 400–500 people have $100 million+ in equity. Almost every employee who joined before SpaceX’s first successful launch in 2008 and simply held their stock crossed that mark. Some saw 17,000x returns.

 About 4,000 more employees who joined later (after 2016) are sitting on at least $1 million each.

 Note SpaceX only has about 15,000 employees. 

 The crazy part: Two engineers can sit next to each other doing the exact same job on the same rocket engine. The one who joined in 2007 can easily be worth 100 times more than the one who joined in 2017. Same work. Different start date + holding period.

 Investing Tip: Get In Early and Hold Onto Winners This is the real lesson. The biggest wealth in transformative companies almost always goes to the people who show up early (as an employee with equity or as an investor buying shares) and then actually hold through the volatility.

 Most people do the opposite: they wait until the company is already huge and “safe,” then buy late and sell at the first big dip. That’s how you miss the life-changing money.
