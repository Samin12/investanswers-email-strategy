 Solana is becoming the fast, cheap settlement layer for trillions in real economic activity — tokenized assets, stablecoins, AI agents, and consumer apps. That creates structural, long-term demand for SOL which will drive price up.... 

 Here are the four demand buckets:

 1. Real-World Assets (RWAs) — $525B SOL demand (the giant) Trillions in stocks, bonds, real estate, and commodities get tokenized on Solana. To trade, provide liquidity, stake validators, and settle, participants must hold/lock SOL as collateral and fuel. Solana taking a solid slice of a $50T tokenized world = half a trillion+ in permanent SOL demand.

 2. Stablecoin Settlement — $150B Trillions flow through stablecoins on Solana. Every transaction burns SOL, and liquidity providers, market makers, and bridges must hold SOL. Even a tiny reserve ratio on massive volume creates ongoing buying pressure.

 3. AI Agents — $27B (biggest upside wildcard) Autonomous AI bots are already trading and paying on Solana (it’s winning ~50-70% of agent activity). They need SOL for gas, priority fees, wallets, and staking. As millions of agents scale, this bucket can grow far beyond the base case.

 4. Consumer Economy (Launchpad + DePIN) — $50B trading, launchpads, decentralized physical networks (Helium, Render, etc.), and everyday swaps are extremely SOL-heavy. Most routes go through SOL pairs and require staking. This is already real and massive today.

 Total structural demand: ~$752 billion worth of SOL that needs to be bought and held for the system to function at global scale.

 With ~578 million SOL circulating, this kind of real usage (institutions, AI, stablecoins, traders) creates relentless buying pressure and locks supply away. As these trends mature over the next 5 years, the market prices in that utility — pushing SOL significantly higher.

 $1,000 is the target if the demand plays out.
