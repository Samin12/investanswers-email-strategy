 Most people chase hot stocks or try to time the market. The real edge?

 Build a simple multi-year plan. Save aggressively. Invest consistently. And actually stick to it — especially when it gets ugly. As I say - "have a plan and stick to it"

 Our New Zealand friend (now 45) is a perfect example. Back in 2016 he built a 10-year plan to reach $1M+ by investing in Tesla. He committed to living very cheaply — so he could DCA roughly 50% of his take-home pay.

 Early years: He was putting away about NZ$25,000–35,000 per year (~US$17k–24k) into Tesla.

 When Tesla was cheap (split-adjusted ~US$14–23), that bought him hundreds to over 1,000 shares per year.

 Later years: Share prices were higher, so he bought fewer new shares… but the ones he already owned compounded massively.

 He had one nasty down year in 2022 (the big drawdown I call “the $106 gift” - I just converted a ton of leaps from this gift on Thursday). Most people panic-sell or stop investing during those periods. He kept going.

 Result? He turned modest savings + iron discipline intomillion dollar wealth — far ahead of his original plan. He started with almost nothing and is now only 45.

 Here’s what he said: “Tesla has exceeded my expectations, but for different reasons… I doubt I can continue compounding at this rate. But if I did, man, what a wild life. I came from pretty much nothing. I think 5-10x in the next 5-10 years is entirely possible though.”

 The Rule Summary: Create a realistic multi-year plan. Save a high percentage of your income (he did 50% by keeping lifestyle costs low). DCA consistently into quality assets. Ignore the noise and keep compounding — especially in the down years.

 The magic isn’t in predicting the future perfectly. It’s in having a plan and having the discipline to execute it for a decade.

 That’s how people build extraordinary wealth.
