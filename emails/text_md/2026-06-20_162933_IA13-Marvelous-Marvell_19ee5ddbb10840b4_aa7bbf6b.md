 A quick note on how the IA13 are all merging ie MRVL NVDA SPCX and TSLA

 Why Marvell (MRVL) looks "Marvelous" and will be a trillion-dollar market cap in the coming years aka a 4x. 

 When Matt Murphy became CEO, the company was stuck making low-margin chips for hard drives, printers, and toys — exactly the wrong place as the cloud and AI era began. He killed those businesses and went all-in on data infrastructure. That single decision changed everything. 

 Marvell went on a smart shopping spree:

 Cavium (network processors)

 Avera (IBM’s custom chip team — unlocked Amazon, Microsoft, Google deals)

 Inphi (optical tech)

 Innovium (Ethernet switching)

 Celestial AI (photonic fabric inside the chip package)

 He made some very clever acquisitions to position MRVL perfectly for the AI explosion. NVDA saw this also and invested $2 billion into the company as part of a strategic partnership. When the king of AI publicly backs you like that, you know it is synergistic 

 The Secret Sauce - They’re Solving AI’s Biggest Bottleneck Right now, AI scaling is limited by connectivity, not compute or memory. You can have the best chips in the world, but if they can’t talk to each other fast enough, the whole system slows down. Marvell specializes in making chips communicate at insane speeds. Enter Photonics, traditional copper wires inside servers get slow and hot when data has to travel. Marvell’s technology uses light (optics) instead. Light moves data faster, farther, and with less power. This is the key to building much larger, more efficient AI systems.

 Nvidia’s upcoming Vera Rubin platform is using Marvell’s Spectrum-X Ethernet Photonics — the first co-packaged optics switch in commercial production. This isn’t “maybe someday” — it’s happening now. I reckon NVDA could even end up acquiring Marvell in the future. 

 PS SpaceX and Tesla are already signed up to buy 20% of all VERA RUBINS....which means they are also buying Marvell tech. 

 Final note, TAM & Mkt Size The optical interconnect market alone is projected to hit $200 billion per year by the end of the decade. But if Marvell’s “data center without distance” vision becomes reality, their addressable market becomes every single connection inside every data center on Earth. That’s a much, much larger TAM.
