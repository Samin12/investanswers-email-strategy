 As we know in about 5 years we can hit escape velocity for longevity. We just need to get to 2030+

 Here are some super easy tips to tackle the biggest killers. Can you spot the ONE common antidote that works across almost all of them? 

 1. Atherosclerotic Cardiovascular Diseases (dark purple) #1 killer by a mile — especially after age 60. This covers heart attacks, clogged arteries, and related heart disease.

 Prevention: Walk ~10,000 steps a day, eat mostly real food (veggies, fruit, nuts, fish, NO junk), 

 2. Cancer (purple) Clear #2 overall. Big impact from age 50 onward.

 Prevention: Get screened on schedule. Early detection is a game-changer.

 3. Neurodegeneration and Dementia (light purple) Rises sharply after 75–80. Includes Alzheimer’s and other dementias.

 Prevention: Move daily, stay socially active, learn new things, eat berries/fish/leafy greens, and keep blood pressure under control. 

 4. Diabetes and Kidney Diseases (beige) Steady contributor in middle and older age.

 Prevention: Cut sugar and ultra-processed carbs, move every day, keep a healthy weight, and get blood sugar checked 

 5. Chronic Respiratory Diseases (pale beige) COPD, emphysema, chronic bronchitis, etc. Smaller but consistent in older groups.

 Prevention: Never smoke (or quit today). Avoid secondhand smoke and bad air. Stay active 

 7. Accidents (gray) Road crashes, falls, etc. More noticeable in younger adults and some elderly falls.

 Prevention: Get FSD. Avoid dangerous situations.
