 This time from Bank of America

 Everyone keeps going back to railroads neary 200 years ago....Unlike railroads, which ran into hard physical limits on how many tracks you could profitably lay and how much freight or passengers actually needed to move once the big routes were built, AI faces what looks like infinite demand for intelligence. 

 Intelligence is a tool that raises productivity in every industry at once (drug discovery, factory robots, personalized education, logistics, creative work, scientific research), keeps getting cheaper and better to deploy, and can even improve itself. That’s why many investors argue you simply cannot compare the current concentration to past bubbles: railroads and dot-com telecom had clear ceilings, while smarter AI can keep creating new value for decades. 

 At the same time, the chart’s 40% reading is flashing and as I have warned there will be casualties along the way......
