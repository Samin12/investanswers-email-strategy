 May 5, 2026 Earnings Call... BOMBSHELL Today Strategy released its Q1 2026 results and held its earnings call. They now hold a record 818,334 Bitcoin (worth about $64 billion at current prices). They bought a lot more BTC this year using money raised from selling new shares and especially their new preferred stock product called STRC.

 BOMBSHELL Michael Saylor and CEO Phong Le are openly talking about strategically selling small amounts of Bitcoin — not as a last resort or panic move, but as a normal, planned tool to help pay dividends on their preferred stock (especially STRC).

 This is a noticeable change in tone. For years Saylor hammered "we will never sell Bitcoin." Now they're saying things like:

 They'll sell BTC "when it's advantageous to the company."

 They might sell a little "just to inoculate the market" and prove they have options.

 They have a math model (they call it the "BTC breakeven ARR") where, if Bitcoin grows even modestly (around 2%+ per year in their example), they can sell tiny bits of BTC to pay dividends and still end up with more Bitcoin overall — as long as they keep issuing new preferred stock fast enough.

 They're basically saying: "We have multiple levers (sell new common stock, sell more STRC preferred, use cash reserves, or sell a little BTC). We're not locked into one path anymore."

 Yes and Rem They Did Sell Bitcoin Before — But Only for Taxes Back in December 2022 they sold a small batch (~704 BTC) at a loss specifically to harvest tax losses and offset gains elsewhere. They immediately bought even more BTC back. It was a pure tax optimization move (crypto isn't subject to the same "wash sale" rules as stocks). 

 Here's the tax trick they're using (I reckon) Strategy tells investors (and the IRS via Form 8937) that they expect zero accumulated earnings & profits (E&P) for the foreseeable future — possibly 10+ years.

 Because of that, the monthly distributions on STRC (and their other preferred) are treated as Return of Capital (ROC) — not taxable income right now. It just reduces your cost basis in the shares.

 This is a huge selling point for income investors.

 If Strategy starts selling Bitcoin at a profit (which they almost certainly would, given their low average cost basis), those gains create taxable earnings. That creates E&P. Once you have E&P, the preferred dividends stop being nice tax-deferred ROC and start getting taxed as regular dividends.

 So the company is kind of boxed in:

 Keep issuing tons of new stock/preferred forever → heavy dilution, but preserve the ROC tax treatment.

 Or sell some BTC to fund things more cleanly → more flexible, but risk blowing up the tax story that makes STRC attractive.

 Saylor & team are trying to thread the needle by saying they'll only do small, strategic sales when it still grows their Bitcoin holdings overall. They're laying the groundwork now so it doesn't shock the market later.

 Is This "Desperation"? No it's more like maturing the model. Will the market understand? Some may not get it. 

 Rem in LIFE NEVER SAY NEVER - unless you really mean it.
