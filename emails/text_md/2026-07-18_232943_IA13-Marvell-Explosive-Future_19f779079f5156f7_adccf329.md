 Note MRVL is 3.3% of my portfolio and a key part of IA13.

 Marvell has been violent both up and down, looks like it has turned the corner at $175 to head back up again. 

 Marvell has grown fast — from $2.3 billion in revenue in 2017 to $8.7 billion now. Analysts expect it to nearly triple again to $23 billion by FY29, driven by exploding AI data center demand.

 They’re playing both sides of the AI boom:

 Designing custom AI chips for big tech (Amazon, Microsoft, Google, etc.)

 Supplying the high-speed connections, switches, and memory tech that link everything together inside data centers.

 Data centers already make up ~76% of their sales, and growth is accelerating.

 At a ~42x forward P/E and $166B market cap, the stock looks expensive — but if they hit these aggressive growth targets, today’s price could look like a bargain in a few years.

 In addition, Morgan Stanley now expects the CXL memory controller (MXC) chip market to explode. Marvell is the standout beneficiary because it already sells the exact chips this upgraded forecast is about.

 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 Risks: Heavy reliance on a few big hyperscaler customers!
