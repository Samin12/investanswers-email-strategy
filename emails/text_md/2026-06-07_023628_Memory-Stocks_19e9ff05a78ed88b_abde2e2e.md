 The AI Supply Crunch AI needs massive amounts of fast memory chips (HBM) to work.

 The chairman of SK Group (which owns SK Hynix, a HBM supplier) just said the bottleneck will likely last through 2030. Even if the whole industry doubles production capacity by then, demand could still be over 5x higher.

 Nvidia’s upcoming AI PCs will need even more of this memory, keeping pressure on supply for years.

 Yet memory stocks (like Micron - $MU) just got dumped after a jobs report. Doh! 

 The crazy part? They’re still much cheaper than other AI stocks:

 $MU trades at ~8x earnings

 The market is still pricing memory like an old cyclical business. But AI has turned it into critical infrastructure.

 NFA If AI spending stays strong, this looks more like a buy-the-dip opportunity than a structural bear case — because the supply shortage isn’t going away anytime soon.
