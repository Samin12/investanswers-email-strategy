 TLDR Work more as early as you can and invest as fast as you can.... 

 Not sure I ever only worked 40 hours a week - more like 60 to 90... but this was interesting. 

 I decided to map this to GDP per Capita to see what falls out. 

 Productivity & choice: Richer countries have much higher output per hour. Workers and societies can achieve high living standards with fewer hours.

 Institutions & culture: Many high-income European countries (Germany, Denmark, Netherlands, etc.) have strong unions, strict working-time laws, generous paid leave, and cultural emphasis on work-life balance.

 Mostly wealthy Northern/Western European countries (Germany at 25.6, Denmark, Netherlands, Norway, Austria, Sweden, etc.). These combine high GDP per capita with strong labor institutions and part-time cultures. 

 Right side (highest hours, ~34–37): Includes the US, South Korea, Israel, Poland, and some others. The US stands out as a high-GDP outlier with relatively long average hours (less vacation - i basically never take vacation, weaker mandated leave). South Korea and Israel show high hours despite solid income levels, reflecting cultural and structural factors.

 Ireland sits in the middle of the pack for hours worked (31.8) but has one of the highest GDP per capita metrics globally ($159,129), heavily skewed by the presence of multinational corporations headquartering there for tax purposes. Not sure there is much trickle down economics there.
