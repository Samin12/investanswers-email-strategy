 TLDR Never let cash pressure force you into trades you wouldn’t normally make. 

 When you’re desperate to raise money, you almost always make suboptimal decisions. Stay disciplined. Raising cash is important, but doing it in a panic will cost you far more in the long run.

 The Details The danger with covered calls is that sometimes the stock takes off and you get stuck on the wrong side of the trade. This is what I call jumping in front of a freight train or being caught offside. 

 That’s exactly what happened to me here. The stock (ALAB) just kept climbing and wouldn’t stop. 

 I had long-term call options (LEAPs) as my main position for protection. To help pay for those LEAPs, I sold PUTS. 

 The PUTS I sold fall in value which is great as I am short. I will cover the short ports to free up a ton of margin and also take a lot of profit. 

 The LEAP calls go up in value which is also great. I want to hold onto as many of these as I can. 

 The calls I sold short go up in value too. That means I have capped part of the money I am making on the LEAP CALLS. Put another way, because I own the long-term LEAP calls, I’m still protected overall. However, by selling those calls, I’m capping how much I can make if the stock keeps rising.

 Since I got caught offside and I really want to keep my LEAPs, I’m going to have to sell some of them to cover the short calls I sold. 

 Bottom line: For every 100 of my long-term LEAP call options, I’ll need to sell about 12 of them. That means by Friday I’ll be selling roughly 12% of my ALAB position. Just wanted to give you a heads-up. I was desperate to raise cash, and in that desperation I took on more risk than I normally would. That was a mistake.

 Same thing happened with the Google call I sold — I covered it too early. I still made money, but I left a lot on the table.

 Big takeaway: When you’re under pressure to raise cash or forced to make trades quickly, you almost always make rash decisions that sometimes cost — even after 36 years in the markets - I know the rules but the cash money raising gun to my head makes me screw up on occasion.
