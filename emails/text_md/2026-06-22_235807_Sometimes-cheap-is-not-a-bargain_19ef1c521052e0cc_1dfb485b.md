 Sometimes "cheap" is not a bargain — it's a warning sign.

 Re the diff between Crowdstrike and Accenture is AI makes strong cybersecurity more necessary, not less. Smarter attacks, bigger data targets, and higher stakes all increase demand for companies like CrowdStrike that stop breaches. It’s a tailwind. 

 The other side of the coin is not the same. Right now, solid well run companies like Adobe, Accenture, Intuit, and PayPal are trading at very low PEs compared to what they earn - they are all trading at a SUB 10 Price to Earnings Ratio. 

 On paper they look like deals. But AI isn't a temporary dip — it's a long-term shift that will eat into their core businesses for years: automating design work, replacing consultants, simplifying taxes and accounting, handling payments, and running customer systems with smart agents.

 When the future earning power of a company gets permanently reduced, a low price today can still be expensive in the long run.

 Don't just buy because the number on the screen is small. Ask why it's cheap first. A low price only helps if the business can still grow and protect its profits despite big changes like AI.
