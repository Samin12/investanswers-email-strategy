 Retail investors have never traded this many options on semiconductor stocks in HISTORY. The volume is nearly 3 times higher than normal. This is an extreme, unprecedented level of retail options activity on semis.

 We here are guilty of this.

 The danger I see is Citadel Track this... and they may try make a play against us. Let me explain this one. 

 Citadel Securities (is also a big options market maker) does see this flow in real time. They are usually the counterparty on the other side of retail options trades.

 When retail floods in buying calls:

 Citadel (and other market makers) end up short those calls.

 To hedge, they buy the underlying stocks as prices rise. This can amplify upside moves.

 But if the move reverses, they have to sell stock to hedge → this can accelerate drops.
