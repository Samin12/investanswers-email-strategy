 The "capex boom is eating all the free cash flow" story is real вҖ” and the bears are using it aggressively right now. Big tech companies like Google (GOOG) are in a massive spending spree. They're pouring huge amounts of money into building data centers, servers, and AI infrastructure (called capex ). This spending is so big that it's swallowing up almost all their free cash flow вҖ” the actual money left over after running the business. Whether Google can grow fast enough to justify the spending and the high valuation is the real question.

 The Bear Case Google is trading at an all time crazy-high 133x free cash flow multiple. Before COVID, it was only around 20x. That's a huge jump, and the actual cash flow hasn't grown much since 2021.

 In 2025, Google Cloud made $59 billion in revenue. But in 2026, they're planning to spend $175вҖ“185 billion on capex. That's spending 3x their cloud revenue just to build stuff.

 AI Overviews (Google's new AI answers that appear at the top of search) are cannabalizing their ad business because people get answers without clicking on ads.

 Investment firm GQG are screaming: "This is unsustainable! Google is spending way more than it makes, and the stock is way too expensive."

 The Counter-Argument The drop in free cash flow isn't because Google is becoming less profitable. It's almost entirely because they're spending so much on growth right now. Their actual operating cash flow is still huge.

 Google is already putting ads inside the AI Overviews, so they're not losing all that ad money.

 Unlike 2022 (when Google stock crashed), Google now has a fast-growing cloud business that's becoming a real second engine.

 Bottom Line Both sides have a point. Google is still an incredibly strong company. But spending this much money means there's very little room for error. The stock price leaves almost no margin of safety if things don't go perfectly. Expect the FUD to also hit META, AMZN, MSFT and even TSLA (although they are spending a lot less).
