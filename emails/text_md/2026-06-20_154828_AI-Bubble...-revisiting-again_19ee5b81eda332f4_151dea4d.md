 In 2000, tech stocks were in a speculative bubble — prices and valuations detached from reality. Today, while tech stocks have performed very well, the rise has been much more grounded. Valuations in many cases are bonkers cheap eg Micron 7, NVDA half of what Costco is etc. 

 In 2000 (the dot-com bubble):

 Investors were paying crazy high prices for tech stocks relative to their expected future earnings.

 Valuations (Forward P/E) went parabolic — up 300%.

 Stock prices went absolutely vertical — up nearly 800%.

 This was classic bubble behavior: people were buying on hype, not fundamentals.

 AI Today:

 Tech valuations have not been exploding. In fact, they've been relatively flat or even slightly down.

 Stock prices have gone up nicely (+160%), but it's nowhere near the insane levels of 2000.

 The growth has been much more reasonable and sustainable.
