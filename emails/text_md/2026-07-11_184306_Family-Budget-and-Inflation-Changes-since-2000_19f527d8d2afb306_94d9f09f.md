 How inflation has impacted spending over the last 25 years - and why many families feel squeezed on the "big three" (housing + healthcare + education/childcare) even as their TVs, phones, and clothes got dramatically cheaper/better. 

 Necessities Share (Today) is now 84.5% of the avg family budget. It was 64% in 2020. It has gone up 20.5% in 25 years. 

 Housing is up to 40% of family budget up from 32%

 Food is 17% - up from 14%

 Childcare nearly 10% up from 6%

 Medical now 9% up from 6%

 Education now 7% up from 4%

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 How to save more?

 Do geographic arbitrage re housing

 Eat at home... 

 Hi deductible health plans and prevention is worth a fortune

 Recruit family members for childcare etc
