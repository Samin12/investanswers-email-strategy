 Sun Tzu said: “Know your enemy and know yourself, and you can fight a hundred battles without disaster.”

 Elon just lived it.He merged xAI into SpaceX → created SpaceXAI (a $1.25T rocket + AI beast). Built the world’s biggest AI supercluster in Memphis (Colossus 1 — 220k+ GPUs). Upgraded to Colossus 2 for himself, leaving Colossus 1 idle.

 Then he did the ruthless genius move: Leased the entire thing to Anthropic (Claude AI — OpenAI’s #1 rival).

 Why this is pure Art of War: Remove the enemy’s bottleneck. Anthropic was choking on compute. Elon hands them the keys. Claude instantly gets 2x+ power, no more rate limits. Their new coding tool explodes.

 Make your rival crush your real enemy. While suing OpenAI in open court (and winning headlines), Elon fuels Anthropic to eat OpenAI’s lunch. “The enemy of my enemy…”

 Turn idle weapons into cash + intel. Spare GPUs = instant revenue. Huge paying customer = IPO rocket fuel for SpaceX.

 Know your enemy from the inside. Elon met Anthropic’s leaders, passed them through his “evil detector,” and now watches exactly how they train at planetary scale. Pure battlefield reconnaissance.

 The Cursor trap (perhaps the sneakiest move). SpaceX already owns a big stake + buyout option in Cursor — the hottest AI coding app that runs on Claude which was also Compute Constrained. Elon now powers Anthropic and sits inside its biggest customer. He sees every dev’s real-world usage patterns, loves, hates, weaknesses. Textbook “know your enemy.” Later he can flip Cursor to Grok models and steal the whole channel while they were using his GPUs the whole time.

 The Playbook Elon had spare rocket-fuel compute sitting around.

 He rented it to his rival so they could destroy his bigger rival (OpenAI).

 Makes money, juices his IPO even more, scouted their playbook, and quietly positioned himself to own the future of AI coding.

 This is classic 4D chess.

Several moves ahead.

Classic Elon.
