 When I was a kid the dream of being a millionaire was simply unthinkable. Fast Forward more than 50 years it has become somewhat commonplace. 

 Here are some charts on how the number of millionaires by country as % of population has changed. NOTE - this data takes the number of millionaire individuals in a country and divides it by the total number of households. ItвҖҷs not a per-citizen (individual) statistic вҖ” itвҖҷs framed around household units, which typically represent families or economic family units living together.

 THE DATA A Massive Global Surge: Every single country on the list saw a significant increase in its millionaire population over the last 9 years.

 Highest Concentrations: Wealth hubs like Switzerland (28.65%), Hong Kong (24.88%), and Singapore (22.83%) now have incredibly dense populations of millionairesвҖ”meaning more than 1 in 5 people there hit the threshold.

 Major Economies: The United States saw its millionaire rate jump from roughly 13% to 18%, while mainland China saw a massive relative percentage jump, going from 0.26% to 1.21%.

 First chart above shows you the % of population who are millionaires eg in USA 9 years ago 12.96% of Americans were Millionaires now it is 17..96%. 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 The second chart above shows you the growth rate in number of millionaires. Eg Hong Kong grew the fastest. CH second, Australia third, Singapore, Netherlands, Canada and so on. 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 THE REAL REASON FOR THE EXPLOSION Debasement (the devaluation of fiat currency) is the primary driver of this trend. Here is why it happens:

 Over the last decade, central banks globally have vastly expanded the money supply. As more money chases a fixed pool of assets, the nominal price of scarce assetsвҖ”like real estate, stocks, and cryptoвҖ”skyrockets. Why i always say be in hard assets. 

 The Nominal Threshold: Being a "millionaire" is a static, nominal metric ($1,000,000). As inflation drives up the paper value of a standard suburban home or a basic retirement portfolio, millions of ordinary people are pushed over that 1-million mark.

 Illusion of Riches: While some of this growth is undoubtedly due to actual technological and economic productivity, a huge portion is simply the measuring stick (the currency) shrinking. A person whose home went from $400k to $900k might now be a millionaire on paper, but they don't necessarily have twice the purchasing power or standard of living they had in 2015.
