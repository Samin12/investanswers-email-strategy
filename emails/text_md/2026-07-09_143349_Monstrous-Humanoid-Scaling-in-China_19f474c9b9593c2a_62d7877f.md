 China is racing to build a huge number of humanoid robots — almost none in 2022, then 20,000 last year, with plans for over 100,000 this year.

 But most of this push isn’t coming from regular companies that need robots to save money. It’s driven by the Chinese government:

 They’re ordering state-owned companies to buy and use 10,000 robots by the end of this year.

 They’re handing out massive subsidies (over $20 billion recently).

 Big state firms like China Mobile and State Grid are placing big orders.

 The biggest real-world use so far (without government pressure) is just a few hundred robots at one factory.

 Tesla gets overlooked here, but they’re the best at actually building things at huge scale, running factories efficiently, and using real AI that could make robots genuinely useful and affordable — without government orders!
