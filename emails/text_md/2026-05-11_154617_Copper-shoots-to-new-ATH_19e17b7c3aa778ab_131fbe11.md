 I entered a massive SL on this one a few months back and it is doing well. We knew there would be a shortage for the next 5 years or more w the electrification of the world but now it is next level. Sulphuric acid shortage was NOT on my bingo card. 

 The main reason is fear of a supply shortage. Traders are also betting that demand for copper will stay strong in the future. A key problem is the disruption in the Middle East. Since March, the U.S.-Iran conflict has severely reduced exports of sulphur and sulphuric acid from the region.

 That matters because sulphuric acid is used in copper processing. It helps refiners extract and purify copper before turning it into anodes, which are a key step in producing usable copper. Because sulphuric acid has become harder to get, China has stopped exporting it. This has made the shortage worse.

 Chile, the world’s biggest copper producer, has also been hit. Some major copper refiners there have had to reduce production, which means less copper is available.
