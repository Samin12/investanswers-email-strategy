 Core Rule I Always Emphasize - EVERYTHING MEAN REVERTS!!! When a stock shoots up 5x, expect it to pull back some — it rarely goes straight up. The opposite is true too. This is especially true in summer with low volume and slow demand: moves get exaggerated, then mean-reversion snap back rubber band is in play. NEVER chase highs; wait for the pullback.

 Key TA Tools I Use (Simple Version) Support & Resistance: Floors (support) where prices often bounce, ceilings (resistance) where they stall. Tip Watch if Nasdaq/Q's hold support or break down.

 Moving Averages (e.g., 200-day): Key "fair value" lines. Price above = bullish trend; below = caution. Hitting the 200-day (like AVGO) is often a solid buy zone.

 ATR Levels: Measures volatility. Minus 2, 3, or 4 ATR = oversold, good spot to start layering in buys (especially with mean reversion).

 Reversals & Candles: Big down moves followed by strong up candles signal potential bottoms (e.g., Micron's big reversal day).

 Trends & Divergence: Use 4-hour or daily charts. Look for the trend to "round out" or change before going all-in. Let the trend be your friend. 

 This Week Semis got crushed — normal pullback after big runs. Many hit attractive ATR/200-day levels (Micron looked like the best value with huge analyst upside).

 Oil creeping up = macro risk to watch.

 Crypto/Bitcoin: Holding key levels but needs to flatten the 200-day for real bottom confirmation. Watch that 64K magnet I refer to. 

 Strategy: Don't try to catch the exact bottom. Start small ("trickle in" or layer), especially on oversold names. Look at me on SpaceX... i know it is going lower but I dip my toe.
