 Bond yields are sky rocketing in Japan. For 30 years plus or as long as I have tracked they always had cheap money. This is where the systemic risk comes in. Japan isn't isolated—its low-rate regime exported liquidity and capital worldwide for years.

 Higher JGB yields + yen volatility force massive deleveraging — investors sell US stocks, bonds, EM assets, and crypto to repay cheap yen loans, triggering a liquidity crunch.

 Japanese institutions pull money home from US Treasuries and global markets to chase higher domestic yields, starving foreign bond markets of demand and pushing yields higher everywhere.

 Japan’s 250%+ debt trap + banking losses create sovereign/financial stress that could spill over into other markets. 

 The good news, if it does blow up, this is the kinda thing that is great for Bitcoin after the initial dip.... this is what BTC was built for.
