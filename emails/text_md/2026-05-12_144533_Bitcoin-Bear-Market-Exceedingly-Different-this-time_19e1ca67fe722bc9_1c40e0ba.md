 This Bitcoin bear market is playing out VERY differently SO FAR — the old 4-year cycle looks toast. 

 In every previous cycle (the gray/white lines), Bitcoin would rip to a new ATH, then crash 75-85% and stay brutally underwater for 300–700+ days. We’re talking multi-year bear markets with soul-crushing lows like $198 (2015), $3,135 (2018), and $15,460 (2022).

 The orange line (current cycle since the May 2026 $82,200 ATH) started similarly but then diverged hard. Instead of following the pack lower, it bounced back strongly and is sitting at roughly -35% around day 200 — while every prior cycle was already at -50% to -70%+ and still falling.

 It simply hasn’t followed the script. No catastrophic 70-80% drawdown. No multi-year death march. The old halving-driven 4-year rhythm that everyone expected appears broken.

 Bitcoin has matured. Bigger money, ETFs, Saylor and other institutional adoption seem to have changed the game. The bear market still hurts, but it’s a completely different animal this time.
