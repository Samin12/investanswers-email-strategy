 Not sure about you all but I dont have 25 years to wait. 

 Bubbles are dangerous because they make normal people believe prices can only go up and that “this time is different.” Everyone piles in, prices get pushed way beyond what any real profits can justify, and when the story finally breaks, a lot of stocks don’t just dip — they can stay crushed for decades. Investors who bought Intel or Corning at the dot-com peak in 2000 had to wait roughly 25–26 years just to break even on the share price alone. That’s not a bad quarter or a rough year; that’s most of a working life spent waiting to get your money back. I dont have that type of time. 

 That said, I still don’t think we’re in a full-blown AI bubble. There are definitely some frothy pockets and companies that look shaky — Oracle is one I’m not convinced is going to make it, and I’ve been calling BS on OpenAI’s financials and leadership for a long time — but the broader picture still feels different from pure 1999-style speculation.

 Winners will take most and continue to crush it... but beware trading around the edges
