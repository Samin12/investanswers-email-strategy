 Back in the '90s I learned the hard way: Don't chase. Replace. 

 Chasing parabolic moves like MU's usually ends in tears. The risk/reward gets ugly fast.

 Getting a ton of questions on Micron — "Why not ditch MSTR for MU?"

 Two reasons I'm staying put:

 I don't chase. I believe in mean reversion. This chart might keep ripping for a while, but reversion will show up at some point — even if it's brief.

 UBS has MU at a $1,650 target. At current levels that's still solid upside. But zoom out: A year from now, what's more likely — MSTR to $245 or MU to $1,650?

 Hard to say. Between my allocation rules, the tax hit from selling, and already running heavy AI exposure with max leverage… I'm holding.

 You invest the way that fits your risk tolerance and rules. Not mine.

 Another Q comes up why dont I mention SNDK? Same reason I dont talk Hynix, Samsung, etc. Micron’s HBM exposure puts it on a meaningfully higher tier than a pure NAND name. My focus is to pic the leader in the space.
