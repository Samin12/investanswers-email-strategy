 Key things I learnt today from Nikesh Arora, Chairman & CEO of Palo Alto Networks (PANW) 

 SAASPocalype - this was genius Analytical SaaS is dead. Enterprises have plenty of data, but they lack the context and memory that AI agents actually need. To make agents work properly, companies will need to consolidate all their data in one place. They can do that with AI but Analytical SAAS is redundant now. 

 Infrastructure SaaS is undervalued. Databases and data platforms (MongoDB, Databricks, Oracle, etc.) will see explosive demand. Arora expects enterprises to need 10x more data storage over the next THREE years to support AI workloads. These infrastructure layers are positioned to benefit significantly.

 System-of-record companies (Salesforce $CRM, etc.) are about to be rewired or killed off. AI agents will largely replace traditional sales workflows. Instead of humans making calls and manually entering data, agents will listen to conversations, understand intent, and directly update systems without humans and without any interface. The era of the interface is dead. This eliminates data entry and traditional UIs. The winners won’t just add AI features — they’ll let agents fundamentally change how work gets done . CRM companies, HR companies etc could all get disrupted here. 

 Security Software: He did not need to mention it... as he spoke of AI risks and how Security is more important than ever... but he did not talk his own book directly. 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 On Security Risks AI red teaming changes the threat landscape. Foreign state actors using advanced AI models (like Anthropic’s Mythos) can create serious economic damage. 89% of breaches still happen because of compromised passwords.

 When Palo Alto Networks tested Mythos, it produced a ~30% false positive rate. That’s excellent for attackers (it finds real vulnerabilities fast) but terrible for defenders. In many cases, there wasn’t even a vulnerability to patch in the first place. This shcoked me. 

 AI models are becoming a utility layer. You no longer need a 180-IQ person when frontier models can directly attack profit pools. Application companies will arbitrage between different models to solve enterprise problems. However, we’re still waiting for a mature layer of agentic application companies to emerge on top.

 Frontier Model Intellectual Property is inherently insecure. For a long time I have warned that AI Frontier Models have little or no moat and users are nto loyal. The entire intelligence of a frontier model lives in its weights — which can fit on a USB stick. That makes models easy to steal and difficult to truly secure.

 Autonomous vehicles / robotaxi (not on my bingo card he would talk about this The Autonomy market has the potential to scale to a $10 trillion opportunity. aka TESLA but he said Waymo needs to scale faster but i am not sure he knows how much Waymo loses, scaling will only make them lose more. 

 OpenAI needs to sell faster. The race is on to capture the massive profit pools being created. Commercial execution will matter as much as model performance. He sees OpenAI as falling behind. The money is in the enterprise not in retail.
