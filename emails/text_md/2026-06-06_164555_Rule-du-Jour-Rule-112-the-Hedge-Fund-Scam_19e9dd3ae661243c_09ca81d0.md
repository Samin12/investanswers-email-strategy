 TLDR High fees + long lockups = one of the most reliable wealth transfer mechanisms ever invented. Don’t be the transferor. BE A COMPOUNDER

 I’ve had so many offers over the years to get into SpaceX, Anduril, xAI, and similar private names. The terms were almost always the same toxic cocktail: 2–2.5% management fee, 20% profit share, and a 3-to-5-year lockup (sometimes longer). Let me walk you thru why IMHO this is the kiss of death. PS Got the same terms this week multiple times at the all in liquidity event in Yountville. 

 The DEAL Imagine at At age 25 you hand a hedge fund manager $100,000. He delivers a 7% average annual gross return.

 Add the standard “2 and 20” — 2% management fee + 20% performance fee — and the outcome is brutal.

 After 40 years (age 65):

 No fees : Your $100K grows to ~$1,497,446 

 After 2/20 fees (modeled correctly year-by-year): You end up with only ~$480,102 

 That’s more than $1 million extracted in fees from a single $100K account. The managers and their partners make millions on your back while you take all the risk. YOU LOSE, THEY WIN

 Why I never took the so called "DEAL" I don’t like locking my money up — not even for a day. Because I could be dead in three years. 

 Second I understand how even a quiet 2% fee can destroy your returns - kinda like taxes

 I know I can outperform hedge funds. 

 The Rule: Never voluntarily hand your capital to a vehicle that charges high fees and locks it up for years. Liquidity is freedom. In most cases you’re far better off owning high-conviction assets directly in public markets where you control your money every single day and pay little to nothing in fees and even sell calls on top.
