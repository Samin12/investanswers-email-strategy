 Jacob recaps the week of June 1-5, 2026: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=j0bxPKjAWw0 

 We just experienced a scary, high-volume sell-off, with the Nasdaq taking a massive hit today. But remember, ive been calling for this for nearly a month and I hedged prematurely... ah well timing is brutally difficult. The S&P was up for nine consecutive weeks—we needed a pullback to revert to the mean. This is not a bubble bursting; it’s a healthy reset. Markets don't go up in a straight line.

 As we head into the summer, expect some sideways, choppy price action. This is a beautiful thing. It gives us a chance to build our positions and act like snipers in our key buy zones before the next leg up. Keep your powder dry, let the dust settle, and wait for the market to come to you.

 Semiconductors & AI 

 AVGO (Broadcom): Down 22% because the CEO accidentally read the wrong year's notes on the earnings call. A complete overreaction. Crashing through the 50-day moving average presents a huge opportunity. Look for heavy support in the 350–378 zone .

 MRVL (Marvell): The sole massive winner, up 37% this week and added to the S&P 500. Do not chase. Wait for sideways consolidation before taking a position.

 MU (Micron): Already pulled back 20%. As I've noted recently, this asset is still incredibly cheap relative to its $1,500+ price targets. I’d love to be a buyer if it dips into the 650–675 zone .

 NVDA (Nvidia): Getting close to the 50-day moving average at 200. Anything sub-200 is a fantastic, screaming buy. If we see true market panic, sub-190 is an absolute gift.

 ARM: Dropping fast. A 300 level makes a lot of psychological and mathematical sense for a bounce.

 AMD: Drifting lower toward 47. No rush to buy the first dip—let it find the 50-day moving average first.

 TSM: If we get a gift down at 340 , take it.

 Big Tech & Software 

 TSLA (Tesla): Cracking through the 200-day moving average. People are likely selling TSLA to fund their heavily oversubscribed SpaceX allocations. Look for support around 360–380 . 

 GOOG (Google): The short-term uptrend is broken after cracking 380 support. Let it roll over and find a bottom before jumping in.

 PLTR (Palantir): Rejected perfectly off the 200-day moving average after the Trump drone news spike. Bias is to the downside here—a target of 100 makes sense.

 Crypto & Quantum 

 BTC (Bitcoin): Trending down in a risk-off market. Sentiment is poor. We could see lower. Do not guess the bottom. Wait for a massive, high-volume capitulation wick—a huge liquidation event—to signal the selling is finally over.

 SOL (Solana): If the market truly panics, look for a deep value entry in the 45 range.

 The Bottom Line: Don't catch falling knives. Let the market panic, wait for the charts to show sideways consolidation, and deploy your capital surgically.
