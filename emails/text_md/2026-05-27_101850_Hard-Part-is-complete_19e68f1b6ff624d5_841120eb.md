 Starcloud just solved the hardest part of building data centers in space: moving data quickly between satellites without going through Earth.

 By equipping their satellites with Starlink’s laser terminals, they created a direct space-based network. This lets AI servers run inference (and eventually training) directly in orbit instead of sending everything back to Earth.

 Powered by nonstop sunlight and cooled by the vacuum of space, these orbital computers avoid land, water, and power limits on the ground. SpaceX has already filed plans for up to a million of them.

 The cloud is moving to space — and AI is handling it.
