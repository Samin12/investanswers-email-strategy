 The Fed held rates steady at 3.50–3.75% (exactly as everyone expected). New Chair Kevin Warsh’s first meeting was unanimous on the hold.

 The real news was in the updated forecasts “dot plot” above... some policymakers got noticeably more worried about inflation staying high. They raised their 2026 inflation projections significantly and now expect the fed funds rate to end 2026 higher than today (median around 3.8%). That flips the script from earlier this year — instead of cuts, a rate hike (or hikes) is back on the table by year-end if inflation doesn’t cool.

 Warsh was measured in the press conference: data-dependent, “trends matter more than single data points,” announced big reviews/task forces (including how AI and productivity affect the economy), and didn’t submit his own rate dot. He emphasized they’re not tightening today and will reassess in ~6 weeks.

 Rate hike potential: 

 Higher than before. Markets now price a 50% chance of one hike before year end. Initial reaction after the statement was classic dump but then AI stocks recovered real fast. They dont seem to care.
