 Jacob recaps the week of June 8-12, 2026: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=glAphOkf3lo 

 SPY and QQQ finished the week down about 1%. They bounced off the 20-day moving average but look like they need more sideways chopping

 Oil is dropping, which is generally good for stocks. Hopes of a Middle East conflict resolution are helping.

 SpaceX IPO’d this week at $135, spiked to $175 intraday, and closed around $163 (up ~1.5% after hours). Strong debut — watch for any spillover effects on related names like TSLA. As per IA trades he believes TSLA is faster horse. 

 ALAB (Astera Labs) +7% this week and making new all-time highs. Added to NasdQ 100 

 MRVL (Marvell Technology) Very strong. It wicks into key support (Level 5) and holds firm after a big rally sparked by positive comments. Resilient price action.

 ARM Exploded +11% today. Breaking out of multi-year highs in classic momentum style. One of the stronger moves of the week.

 MU (Micron) Looks ready to run again toward and past $1,000. Big long-term targets sit at $1,200–$2,000. Memory names are showing good relative strength.

 AMD Down ~20% from its high — right in the normal 20–30% dip range this cycle has seen. Dips have been short-lived, but they often feel overextended when trying to buy the exact bottom.

 AVGO (Broadcom) Big gap down but finding support at key Level 5. It could wiggle a bit lower, but from a value standpoint it looks decent relative to its all-time highs.

 NVDA (Nvidia) Daily trend indicator is about to flip positive (orange), but a deeper drop into the 200-day moving average would be an excellent place to add. That would be a high-conviction accumulation zone.

 TSLA (Tesla) Adding shares this week. Price broke below the 200-day moving average but is bouncing nicely off key Level 5 support and showing signs of higher lows. More downside to that level would create better buying opportunities. SpaceX IPO impact is still TBD.

 TSM (TSMC) Slow, steady grind higher. No major pullback yet to offer a clear entry.

 GOOGL (Google) Pulling back from highs and looking for support at the 50-day moving average. The 4-hour chart has been doing a good job signaling tops recently.

 PLTR (Palantir) Continuing to lag and look weak. Recent video discussed whether this is a buyable dip — it appears to offer decent value relative to its highs.

 BTC (Bitcoin) Coiling just under $64K with wicks above it. A clean break above $64K could spark a relief rally toward $70K if macro/news improves.

 SOL (Solana) Showing relative strength with a double-bottom pattern. Trying to break above $68. Watch for a reclaim of key support levels to confirm the move higher.
