 I debated sharing this since yesterday morning.... it ate me up the last 36 hours. But forewarned is forearmed and I decided to share - and it will trigger a reaction. 

 Ken Griffin (Citadel CEO) admitting he went home Friday “fairly depressed” after seeing his firm’s AI agents do in hours what teams of highly paid PhD-level finance experts used to take months to complete.

 Griffin stressed these aren’t average white-collar jobs — they’re extraordinarily high-skilled roles in market analysis, deal structuring, and complex research. He called it “agentic AI” and said you could already see the dramatic impact it will have on society.

 Elon simply asks: “Where will AI be in 1, 2 or 3 years?”

 Depressing Takeaway Even the boss of one of the world’s most sophisticated hedge funds is visibly shaken by how fast AI is now eating elite, high-IQ work — not just basic tasks. It’s a powerful signal that the change is real, accelerating, and hitting the top of the skill ladder first.

 The Timeline (ESTIMATE) of “Where will AI be in 1, 2 or 3 years?” 1) NOW: AI becomes the default assistant for serious work (coding, research, finance, legal, marketing, operations). Hiring gets compressed — fewer juniors, smaller teams. One strong person + agents can replace several average ones. The people who use it well look superhuman; everyone else looks slow.

 2) YEAR 2 ie 2027 - Real “agentic workflows” become normal inside top companies. AI doesn’t just chat — it pulls data, writes code, tests, summarizes, drafts contracts, screens candidates, monitors markets, etc. Humans become reviewers, commanders, and the “taste/accountability” layer. The old white-collar career ladder starts breaking because the entry-level training ground disappears. 

 3) Year 3 ie 2028 - AI stops feeling like “software” and becomes infrastructure (like electricity). Winners will be companies that own the full agent operating layer, data, workflows, and control systems. 

 AI won’t feel like one more tech product. It will feel like a new economic atmosphere. Everyone will breathe it, but not everyone will own the oxygen.

 Ken Griffin is orth $50bn and he is shaking in his boots.
