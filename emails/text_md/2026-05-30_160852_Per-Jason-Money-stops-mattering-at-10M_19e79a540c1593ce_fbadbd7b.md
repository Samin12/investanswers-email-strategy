 He talks T Bills... but think STRC... 11.5% return on $10M = $1,150,000 per year. 

 At the 5% rate J-Cal referenced: $500,000/year — "real $-U money" you can live off without working.

 At 11.5% : $1,150,000/year — more than double the income on the same capital.

 To match J-Cal’s $500k/year lifestyle using an 11.5% expected return instead of 5%, you’d only need roughly:

 $4.35 million (not $10M).

 In Reality $2–$3M is ample “Start Spending Rule” 

 While $10M parked at a safe 5% is classic “F-U money,” if you’re running a high-conviction portfolio with ~14%+ long-term expected returns, start spending more intentionally once you approach $2–3M .

 Why? 

 At $2M → ~$230k/year expected

 At $3M → ~$345k/year expected

 That’s already enough to fund a very comfortable, low-stress life in most places

 Rem the first $100K is the hardest, the first $1M is a lot easier, after that the Deca comes fast
