 Huawei just said: “Shrinking transistors is running out of gas. Let’s stop wasting time on the roads instead.”

 The problem they’re attacking: In modern AI chips is a ton of time and power is lost not on actual calculations, but on signals traveling long distances through wires between transistors, memory, and other chips. Huawei calls this delay τ (tau).

 Their fix – LogicFolding: They “fold” logic circuits vertically into layers so the most important paths become much shorter. Shorter roads = faster signals, less wasted energy, higher effective density.

 Even on crappy old tech like 7nm-class processes they can hit 1.4nm-equivalent density by 2031. 

 +55% transistor density, ~41% better power efficiency.

 Already built 381 chips this way. First full version drops in new Kirin phone chips this fall.

 Why We Care? Sanctions blocked Huawei from TSMC’s best machines. So they made their own. It could potentially weaken the moat of TSMC, Intel, and Nvidia. I will monitor carefully.
