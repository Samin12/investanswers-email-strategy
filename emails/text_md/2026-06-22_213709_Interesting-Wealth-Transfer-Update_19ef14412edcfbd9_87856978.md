 I went back and forth all day on whether to post this or not. But after hearing a story this weekend about a father with three kids who didn’t get a single Happy Father’s Day message from any of them, it hit different. So felt worth sharing!

 Many had estimated that $84–124T of US wealth would be passed down, but that is changing.

 Why Boomers Are No Longer (Fully) Passing Their Wealth On Key stats from the piece (drawing on Schwab, Northwestern Mutual, Fidelity, etc.):

 45% of wealthy Boomers (> $1M investable assets) prioritize spending their money on themselves during their lifetime (vs. just 15% of wealthy Millennials and 11% of Gen X).

 Only 22% of Boomers plan to leave an inheritance at all; just 11% rank it as their top financial goal.

 40% of Boomers have no will; 17% of those without one are unsure who they'd even leave assets to.

 This aligns with the "GO with zero" mindset — spend it while you can enjoy it (travel, experiences, healthcare) rather than hoard for heirs.
