 Strategy is shifting from just issuing new securities to actively managing its capital — buying things back when they’re cheap and being more disciplined about when to raise new money. They’re keeping a strong cash buffer and Bitcoin holdings while giving themselves tools to improve liquidity and potentially reduce future dividend costs.

 Strategy Inc (MSTR) just announced a new capital management plan on June 29, 2026. Strategy created something called the Digital Credit Capital Framework with 5 parts.

 1. USD Reserve Policy (Cash Buffer) They currently have about $2.55 billion in cash reserves.

 This cash can only be used to pay dividends on their preferred stocks and interest on debt.

 They want to keep at least 12 months of coverage. Right now they have about 17 months.

 If they use the cash, they can later refill it by selling some Bitcoin (under controlled rules).

 2. STRC Dividend Policy They’re raising the dividend rate on their STRC preferred stock to 12% starting July 1, 2026. 

 STRC Shoots up to $80 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 The goal is to make STRC trade close to its $100 face value (they’re aiming for $99–$100 range over time).

 3. Digital Credit Securities Repurchase Program They can buy back up to $1 billion of their preferred stocks (STRC, STRF, STRD, STRK).

 They’ll likely start with STRC if it’s trading at a big discount. 

 4. MSTR Common Stock Repurchase Program They can also buy back up to $1 billion of regular MSTR common shares.

 5. BTC Monetization Program The board approved selling Bitcoin for three specific reasons:

 Build up to $1.25 billion in cash reserves.

 Pay preferred stock dividends or debt interest (if selling Bitcoin is better than issuing new stock).

 Fund the stock buybacks mentioned above.

 They can only sell Bitcoin within these limits. Anything beyond that needs fresh board approval.
