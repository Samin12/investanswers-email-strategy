 OpenAI CFO Friar was very eloquent. In fact she should replace Sam Altman and become CEO. 

 She talked about OpenAI’s strategy, consumer/enterprise balance, compute constraints, and product roadmap (including the Jony Ive collaboration). 

 A few takeaways: 

 Biggest takeaway - the Compute reality: OpenAI and the whole industry is Compute supply-constrained in 2026. She also said if folks say they have compute capacity for 2027 they are full of it. Inference going global for real-time/agent use but uses a truck load of power. Sora was paused due to compute but “video is not dead.”

 When asked about how they focused on Retail and not enterprise and then Anthropic ate their revenue lunch.. they said they are focusing more now on 50/50 split between Enterprise and Retail.

 Jony Ive consumer product: announcement by end of 2026, public early 2027.

 They also say they do humanoid robots - BS IMHO as they do not have any ability to manufacture anything. 

 IPO philosophy: “Not a destination, just a milestone/fundraising tool. Market is a weighing machine, not a popularity contest.”

 Now the same day Anthropic announced their IPO filing... she seemed a tad surprised at that but OpenAI also just raised $130BN - the largest amt ever. 

 OpenAI is building the AI layer/infra with multiple interfaces (ChatGPT as one consumer entry point, plus Codex, Frontier enterprise, etc.). ChatGPT has >900M weekly users; Codex hit 5M weekend active users recently. 

 This one I did not understand... they were proud of their two fastest growing markets Africa and Azerbaijani.

 Gross margins improving dramatically (GPT-4 → 5.4: ~97% cost reduction per token); paid tiers show 2–11x higher engagement. Revenue now roughly 50/50 consumer/enterprise. Mission remains AGI for all humanity, not just payers.

 OpenAI Ads are real- she said “If Google and Meta had a child, it would be ChatGPT” — already taking significant search share.

 Would I buy into the OpenAI IPO? No. Could I buy it today? Yes. But will I? Absolutely not.
