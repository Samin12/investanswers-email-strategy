 The chart beautifully illustrates the current state of the AI infrastructure boom: older GPUs are cheap and plentiful, but the latest and greatest silicon commands a significant scarcity premium because demand is ferocious and the physical supply chain (especially packaging and memory) hasn’t fully caught up yet. IT also shows NVIDIA’s strong pricing power and high utilization while highlighting why power, data-center construction, and advanced packaging have become the new bottlenecks.

 This is a classic cycle story playing out at unprecedented scale: new architecture launches → hype + demand surge → prices spike → supply eventually ramps → effective costs fall and capabilities explode. We’re still early in the “prices spike / supply constrained” phase for Blackwell in mid-2026.

 Note the spike in March was due to the GTC ie GPU Technology Conference NVIDIA’s flagship annual developer and technology conference
