 AI demand is giving semiconductor and memory stocks a huge boost. Their profit margins are rising sharply because so many companies are rushing to build AI systems that supplies of chips are getting tight.

 Morgan Stanley says memory chips have become the biggest bottleneck holding back AI and next-generation computer processors. At the same time, big customers are paying in advance and signing long-term contracts, which gives these chip companies much clearer visibility into future sales.

 Micron tops the list.
