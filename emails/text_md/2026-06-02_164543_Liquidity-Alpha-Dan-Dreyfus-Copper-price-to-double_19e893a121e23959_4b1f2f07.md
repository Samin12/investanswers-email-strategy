 Copper is not just going higher — the setup supports a move that could double (or more) from here over the medium term as the market fully prices in the severity of the supply wall. This is the physical-world bottleneck of the AI + electrification + onshoring supercycle.

 Why?

 Demand is surging from multiple directions at once We have parallel, multi-trillion-dollar buildouts all competing for the same Copper metal:

 Aerospace: $1T+ backlog (Boeing + Airbus over 10+ years)

 Grid (Transmission & Distribution): $1T+ in the U.S. alone over 10+ years

 Power Generation: Another $1T+

 Data Centers + Semiconductors: $1T+ global semiconductor capex + hyperscaler spending

 Defense: U.S. spending rising from 3.3% → 5% of GDP by 2030

 Supply is catastrophically constrained It takes 7–12 years to permit, finance, and build a new world-class copper mine.

 ~0 major new mines are expected to come online globally before 2030.

 Ore grades have already fallen –40% since 1991 (you have to move dramatically more rock and energy for every pound of copper produced).

 Annual global copper production must roughly double just to meet demand.

 To do that, the world needs 5 new world-class mines per year for the next 20 years — 100 new mines by 2045 .

 The supply cupboard is bare, and the mining industry has been under-investing for years. Even at today’s elevated prices (~$6.50/lb as of early June 2026), almost no new large-scale projects are being sanctioned at the required pace. 

 TLDR

 Price going to $13. 

 FYI I own Copper since 2025 (only precious metal I own)
