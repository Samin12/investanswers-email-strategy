 H.T to CS for the point. Here is some clarification. 

 Brokerages like Schwab and Fideilty classify IPO shares as non-marginable securities. 

 This means:

 Even if you have a margin account, you cannot borrow against margin to buy the IPO shares.

 You need sufficient settled cash (or cash available to borrow based on other holdings) in your account to cover 100% of the purchase price for any shares you are allocated.

 Eg in your account you will see something like this.... here is what you can and cannot use and REM do everything in LAYERS>... LAYER IN LAYER OUT DO NOT GO BIG BANG on this IPO as it could dip in subsequen 90 days.
