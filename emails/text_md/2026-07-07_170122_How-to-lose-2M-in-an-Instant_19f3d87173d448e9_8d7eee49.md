 ... and why L2s on ETH and ZKRollups are dangerous

 The wallet (0xff89...981e) pulled roughly 1,126 ETH from Binance in two withdrawals. They then tried to swap it all for $LIT — the native token of Lighter , a legitimate zk-rollup perpetuals exchange that’s been rallying (up ~53% that week, trading around $2.46–$2.60 at the time).

 They used a popular DEX aggregator (0x) to find the “best” route.

 What Actually Happened Instead of getting ~800,000+ LIT tokens (worth roughly their $2M), they received only 5,776 LIT worth about $14,208.

 They effectively paid ~$348 per LIT — roughly 140× the market price.

 The route the aggregator picked went through a tiny, shallow liquidity pool for an intermediate token called AVAIL (via Uniswap V3 → USDC → LIT on Uniswap V4). That thin pool couldn’t handle a $2M order. The price impact was catastrophic.
