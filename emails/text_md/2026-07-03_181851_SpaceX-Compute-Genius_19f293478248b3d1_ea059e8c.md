 Here’s the genius of Elon and SpaceX’s compute strategy

 They invented a brand-new premium market for AI compute. Instead of competing on cheap long-term cloud rentals like everyone else, SpaceX created high-priced, large-scale on-demand deals that charge way more per megawatt.

 Their deals with companies like Google and Anthropic generate 3–4x higher revenue per MW than typical offerings — and even bigger profit margins 

 Smart “flexible but stable” contracts. Deals are structured as 3-year agreements, but either side can cancel with just 90 days’ notice. This gives customers flexibility while still letting SpaceX lock in huge, high-margin revenue streams.

 For others to build and finance these giant clusters fast enough for short-notice, high-value deals requires serious capital and speed. All other “neoclouds” can’t do it. Only Elon can. 

 High-margin optionality + flywheel effect. SpaceX can quickly monetize spare capacity at premium prices, then easily redirect it internally if needed. This turns compute shortages into a profitable, low-risk side business that even big players cant copy.
