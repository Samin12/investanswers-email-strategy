 Cathie Wood sees what most cannot: we are living through the exact opposite of the dot-com bubble.

 Where that era was a fever dream of sky-high valuations chasing vaporware, today we stand at the threshold of something far more profound—an intelligence explosion built on real technology that has already crossed the threshold into viability.

 “We are ready for primetime,” Wood declares, “with five innovation platforms… involving 15 different technologies that are all converging.”

 The setup is quietly revolutionary and u could argue comically undervalued:

 → Valuations sit dramatically below the speculative madness of 1999–2000, even as the underlying capabilities have advanced exponentially.

 → Costs are collapsing at a velocity never before witnessed in human history—compute, energy, data, and intelligence itself becoming dramatically cheaper by the month.

 → The technologies are no longer experimental. They are ready. They work. They scale.

 What the world still calls “AI hype” (michael burry is having a field day w his bearish antics) BUT it is actually the quiet birth of a new economic epoch—one where intelligence becomes free and abundant, creativity is democratized, and the rate of human progress itself begins to compound at machine speed - yeah scary! 

 Most investors are still staring at the rear-view mirror of 2000, terrified of ghosts. I know I lived thru it and traded thru it.... lucky to do things like Short EMC at the top....
