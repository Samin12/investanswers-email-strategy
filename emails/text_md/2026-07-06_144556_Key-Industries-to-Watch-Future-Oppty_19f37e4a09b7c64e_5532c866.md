 Each panel tracks the share of underlying science — basically, what percentage of the key scientific papers, discoveries, and research that later show up in patents and real technologies comes from the United States (blue line) versus China (red line).

 We are moving from a world where the US had a commanding lead in almost all frontier science to one where China is a peer or leader in research output across most critical technologies. This doesn't mean the US is "losing" overnight — it still has advantages in commercialization, capital markets, and certain application layers — but the foundational research base is shifting.

 The big oppty is Robotics... market cap is NOTHING NOW... but will be the largest category in 5 years or so.
